Hi!

I'm a lifelong student of computing currently interested in hardware-software co-design for accelerating AI inference. My research interests include:

- Hardware accelerators (i.e. [TPU](https://www.lockheedmartin.com/en-us/products/sbirs.html), [LPU](https://groq.com/inference/))
- Dataflows mapping computations to processing elements
- Network-on-Chip communication architectures
- Model compression pipelines
- Scalable distributed access to compute resources

I'm currently working as a Software Architect at Lockheed Martin Space where I support projects related to AI path planning, computer vision, and quantum machine learning. I've supported remote sensing missions for [planetary science in deep space](https://science.nasa.gov/planetary-science/programs/new-frontiers/) and for [early missile warning](https://www.lockheedmartin.com/en-us/products/sbirs.html) on Earth.

I'm also a PhD student at CSU where I study hardware-software co-design and [write about model compression](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.02214).

During my time working with remote sensing for missile defense systems I learned the idea of owning a system "from photon to warfighter." This phrase implies extreme end-to-end technical ownership of a system as a means of achieving mission success, and it has stuck with me as a core tenet of my work.

I'm happily married to my [best friend](https://www.instagram.com/malachiwestbrookphotography/). We celebrate our anniversary once every four years (she's my leap day love).

In 2016, we founded and later successfully sold an [award-winning bookstore and coffee shop](https://www.chaptersonmain.com/). In 2022 we bought and remodeled our 100-year-old home in Arkansas. We have a tortoiseshell cat named Evelyn and a few dozen koi fish.
